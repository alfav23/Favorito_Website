Weekly Blog

10.03 a little overwhelmed

So far, the course work is exciting and fun, so I don’t think that it will feel like a chore even as I continue through the semester. I am currently working on some models for my group project in Maya, and I am learning some interesting techniques in Composite. Previsualization is helping me re-center my goals and find out what I need to do to get the most out of my time at Brookdale. I am a little overwhelmed, but ultimately, I am excited to complete a fully rendered short movie. Everyone’s ideas are finally starting to come together into a cohesive plan, despite several disagreements and (small) arguments. This is my first time working as a team on a project like this. As someone who is shockingly terrible at teamwork, it’s not comfortable, but it’s part of the job. I hope to continue learning new skills and making connections with my peers.

10.10 making a trombone

I went in person to a class I normally attend online. It was a nice change of pace, and I got to see parts of the Brookdale campus that I have never seen before. The class is Composite and is in the ATeC building. It is in the basement, so I was a little bit creeped out at first, as I was supposed to be the only one in attendance in person, however it was a nice room and there was a second person there besides me. I had a lot of fun and felt more engaged rather than being at home where I tend to fall asleep in my computer chair. It is a little bit too comfortable and has a lower back massage pillow. We learned how to use sweep, loft, and spline tools in Cinema 4D. I had to go in person because the disk on my computer at home is full. I bought an external drive to add more storage and to open some space to run programs without them crashing consistently because of scratch disk errors. I made a trombone in Cinema 4D for my assignment making use of splines and the related tools. I play trombone and I own one, so I used my own trombone as the model, and it made me very happy and reminded me why I love what I study.

10.17 Buffalo

I went to Buffalo, NY to visit my friend who recently moved there with her husband and kids. I used to tutor her kids in Spanish, and worked with her at a previous job, and I missed seeing them. A couple of mutual friends and I visited and stayed near her apartment which is about ten minutes from Niagra falls. We went to Niagra falls together and explored the city, and barbequed and caught up, and then returned home Tuesday morning. I brought my laptop and was able to code the basis of my website and join a zoom class, but I am trying to cram my remaining larger assignments into the afternoons after work, which I’m just going to pretend is possible (I work from 12:40 am-1:10pm for five days a week #consumerism #daddybezos). I did this to myself; I accept my fate.

10.24 progress

We are making progress on our short movie in Maya. I completed the model for the second creature that lives in the alien world that my group created. I’m hoping that by the end of this week, we can be done with all of the models and start working more on the textures and environment of our world. It looks like we are making good progress. I also have been working on creating complex objects in Cinema 4D and edited my trombone into a video of a music room using color correction and camera tracking among other After Effects tools. After Effects 2025 was released a short while ago and it doesn’t seem much different so far. I want to begin researching the best ways to make 3D environments for webtoons soon to start creating objects and background to make the process easier for my webtoon. I haven’t had much time to draw lately and am a bit sad about it, because I have one piece I stopped working on halfway and want to get back to.
